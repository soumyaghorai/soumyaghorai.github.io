Research Interests

My current research interests lie in single-stage AC to DC conversion circuits, particularly for high-power electric vehicle (EV), plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV), and battery bank chargers. These applications typically require power supplies ranging from tens to hundreds of kilowatts, necessitating the use of either three-phase or single-phase power factor correction (PFC) rectifiers to supply the battery from utility AC mains.

In conventional applications, two conversion stages are employed. A three-phase or single-phase boost-type PFC front end generates ≈ 800 V DC bus voltage, which is subsequently reduced to the battery voltage level via a DC-DC converter, resulting in a two-stage system. Furthermore, complexity enfolds when the nominal voltages of electric vehicle (EV) battery packs currently available in the market range from ≈ 200 - 900 V, necessitating the design of a variable gain AC-DC converter.

An interesting alternative is the single-stage solution, which integrates PFC and high-frequency isolation within a composite structure, thereby reducing the component count. Challenges come to devise a suitable modulation strategy for the single-stage converter which facilitates:

  • Minimization of conduction losses and achieving soft-switching for the devices to enhance overall efficiency.
  • Operational capability across the wide battery voltage range while maintaining a flat efficiency curve.
  • Implementing active power decoupling in single-phase circuits to improve the battery charging current profile by canceling AC ripple at twice the mains frequency.
