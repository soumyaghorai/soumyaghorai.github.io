Warm Greetings!

My research, under the supervision of Souvik Chattopadhay, focuses on bidirectional AC–DC/AC–AC high-frequency power conversion systems, as well as the control and modulation strategy of dual-active-bridge-based converters.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in single-stage AC–DC conversion circuits, particularly for high-power EV and PHEV chargers. These applications typically require inductively isolated single-phase or three-phase AC-to-DC circuits, ranging from 3.3 to tens of kilowatts.

Conventional approaches employ two conversion stages: a PFC rectifier front end and a DC–DC stage. A promising alternative is the single-stage solution, which integrates PFC and high-frequency isolation in a suitably crafted hybrid structure. My work explores suitable modulation strategies that ensure:

  • Modulation strategies ensuring minimum high-frequency current RMS while enhancing soft switching, achieving higher efficiency.
  • Operation across wide battery voltage ranges and wide power range while maintaining nearly flat efficiency profiles.
  • Active power decoupling in the case of single-phase PFC integrated DAB on-board chargers to reduce low-frequency ripple in single-phase chargers. This is necessary for better Li-ion battery health, as reported.
