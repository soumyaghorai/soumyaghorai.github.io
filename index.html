I am Soumya (pronounced Sou-mo), currently pursuing a Doctor of Philosophy (2022-present) in Power Electronics at the Department of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur. My research, conducted under the supervision of Souvik Chattopadhay, focuses on the control and modulation of dual-active-bridge-based AC-DC single-stage converters. Presently, we are engaged in a project aimed at developing a 50kW DC fast charging station for electric vehicles, funded by the Central Power Research Institute (CPRI).
I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering from Jadavpur University, Kolkata, in 2017, and obtained my Master’s degree in 2019 from the same institution, specializing in Energy Science and Technology.
My current research interests lie in single-stage AC to DC conversion circuits, particularly for high-power electric vehicle (EV), plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV), and battery bank chargers. These applications typically require power supplies ranging from tens to hundreds of kilowatts, necessitating the use of either three-phase or single-phase power factor correction (PFC) rectifiers to supply the battery from utility AC mains. In conventional applications, two conversion stages are employed. A three-phase or single-phase boost-type PFC front end generates ≈ 800 V DC bus voltage, which is subsequently reduced to the battery voltage level via a DC-DC converter, resulting in a two-stage system. Furthermore, complexity enfolds when the nominal voltages of electric vehicle (EV) battery packs currently available in market range from ≈ 200 - 900 V, necessitating the design of a variable gain AC-DC converter. In most cases, high-frequency isolation is incorporated into the DC-DC converter to mitigate common mode noise and enhance short circuit protection. An interestig alternative is the single-stage solution, which integrates PFC and high-frequency isolation within a composite structure, thereby reducing the component count. Challenges come in order to devise a suitable modulation strategy for the single-stage converter which felicitates a) Minimizition of conduction losses and achieving soft-switching for the devices to enhance overall efficiency. b) Operational capability across the wide battery voltage range while maintaining a flat efficiency curve. c) Implementing active power decoupling in single-phase circuits to improve the battery charging current profile by canceling AC ripple at twice the mains frequency.
